Młynarski Group

Computational Neuroscience at LMU Munich

We ask what are the functions of neural systems and why are they organized in the way they are. We seek answers by developing normative theories and using these theories to derive computational models. We collaborate with experimental groups and design statistical analyses of neural and behavioral data.

Our primary focus is on sensory systems. We study how the brain infers properties of the environment, represents sensory stimuli and adapts to the constantly changing circumtstances. We rely on tools from information theory, statistics and computer science. Theories at the center of our work are substrate-independent and we seek connections with different domains of biology to identify shared principles.
